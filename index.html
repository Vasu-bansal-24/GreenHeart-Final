What is organic farming and how is it different from conventional farming?

Organic farming is a method of crop and livestock production that avoids the use of synthetic pesticides, fertilizers, and GMOs. It focuses on sustainable practices like crop rotation, natural pest control, and soil health maintenance through organic matter.

How is technology transforming modern agriculture?

Modern agriculture is being revolutionized by technologies like IoT sensors, drones, AI-powered analytics, and precision farming techniques. These tools help optimize resource usage, predict weather patterns, monitor crop health, and increase overall farm productivity.

What are the benefits of implementing smart farming solutions?

Smart farming solutions offer numerous benefits including reduced water usage, optimized fertilizer application, early pest detection, improved yield prediction, and data-driven decision making. This leads to increased efficiency and sustainability in agricultural operations.

How can farmers transition to organic farming using modern technology?

Farmers can use soil testing technology, biological pest monitoring systems, and precision irrigation to support organic farming practices. Modern tech tools help maintain organic certification standards while maximizing crop yields and quality.
